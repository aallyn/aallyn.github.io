# STEPS: From ISEC 2020 #

**Different types of "distribution" models**

Process-implicit: We assume that we have captured the biological and ecological processes in the model.  
    Example: Correlative species distribution models with environmental variables

Process-explicit: We explicitly account for the biological and ecological processes in the model.
    Example: Individual-based models with growth, survival, dispersal parameters.

*See Briscoe et al. (2019) in Ecology Letters*

**STEPS**  

STEPS is open source, freely-available and customizable

STEPS has the capacity to account for...  

1. Population change - Uses transition matrixes with or without added layers for survival and fecundity
2. Population dispersal - Uses kernels or random movement patters
3. Population modification - Uses translocation layers or mortality layers
4. Population density dependence - Uses layers
5. Habitat modification - Uses layers
